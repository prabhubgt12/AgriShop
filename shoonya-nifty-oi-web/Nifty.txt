waht is vwap

ChatGPT said:
VWAP stands for Volume Weighted Average Price.
It is the average price of a stock or index during the day, but calculated by giving more importance to prices where higher volume was traded.

In simple words:

VWAP shows the true average traded price of the day.

Simple example
Suppose Nifty trades like this:

Price	Volume
25,800	1,000
25,820	5,000
25,850	2,000
Normal average:

(25800 + 25820 + 25850) / 3 = 25,823
But VWAP gives more weight to the price with higher volume (25,820).

So VWAP will be closer to 25,820.

What VWAP tells you
VWAP is used to see:

Whether market is bullish or bearish

Whether buyers or sellers are in control

Basic interpretation
Price vs VWAP	Meaning
Price above VWAP	Bullish strength
Price below VWAP	Bearish strength
Price near VWAP	Sideways market
Why traders use VWAP
Large institutions:

Use VWAP as a benchmark price

Try to buy below VWAP

Try to sell above VWAP

So VWAP often acts like:

Dynamic support

Dynamic resistance

How it looks on chart
On intraday chart:

VWAP appears as a single line

It starts from market open

Updates continuously through the day

Simple trading use
Bullish setup
Price above VWAP

Pullback to VWAP

Bounce from VWAP
→ Buy Call

Bearish setup
Price below VWAP

Pullback to VWAP

Rejection at VWAP
→ Buy Put

Why VWAP matters for your algo
You can use it as a trend filter.

Example:

    allow only CE trades

    allow only PE trades
This avoids trading against the trend.
